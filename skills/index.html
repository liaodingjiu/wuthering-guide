Wuthering Waves Skills & Combat

Every character in Wuthering Waves has five ability types. Understanding how they work — and how they chain together — is the difference between clearing content and struggling through it.

⚔️ Ability Types 🔄 Concerto Energy 🔁 Intro & Outro 💨 Dodge & Parry 🌈 Elements 👤 Characters

⚔️ The 5 Ability Types

Every Resonator has exactly five abilities. They always appear in this order on each character detail page →:

⚔️

① Basic Attack

Your bread-and-butter damage. Usually a 4-5 hit combo. Heavy Attack variant consumes stamina for a charged hit. Mid-air attack for plunging damage. This is what you do between skill cooldowns.

✨

② Resonance Skill

Your primary active ability. Short cooldown (8-15s). Generates Concerto Energy on hit. Often enters an enhanced state or provides a buff. This is the skill you spam most often.

💥

③ Resonance Liberation

Your ultimate. Long cooldown (20-30s), high Energy cost (100-150). Deals massive damage in a large area. Always generates significant Concerto Energy. Use it as the climax of your damage rotation.

⚙️

④ Forte Circuit

Your character's unique mechanic. Every Resonator's Forte works differently — Jinhsi builds Incandescence stacks, Camellya accumulates Thorns for a special blooming strike. Read this carefully for your Main DPS.

🔁

⑤ Outro Skill

Triggers when you swap this character out with full Concerto. Usually applies a team-wide buff or deals parting damage. Rotating to trigger the right Outro buff is the core of team building.

💡 Skill leveling priority: Forte Circuit → Resonance Liberation → Resonance Skill → Basic Attack → Outro Skill. Max Forte first — it's the core mechanic everything else scales from. Check each character page → for their specific skill descriptions and cooldowns.

🔄 Concerto Energy

Concerto is the invisible resource that governs team rotations. Every attack and skill generates Concerto. When a character's Concerto gauge fills completely, swapping them out triggers both their Outro Skill and the incoming character's Intro Skill simultaneously.

Key facts:

  • Resonance Skills generate the most Concerto — typically 8-15 per cast. Basic attacks generate small amounts per hit.
  • Resonance Liberation instantly generates 20-30 Concerto — one Liberation often fills the gauge by itself.
  • Support characters build Concerto fastest — their skills are designed to fill the gauge quickly so you can swap back to your DPS. Verina and Shorekeeper can fill theirs in 3-4 seconds.
  • You can see Concerto as a glowing circle around the character icon in the top-right HUD during combat.
🎯 The core rotation loop: Support builds Concerto → swap to Sub-DPS (triggers buffs) → Sub-DPS uses skill + liberation → builds Concerto → swap to Main DPS (triggers more buffs) → Main DPS goes all-out during the buff window → repeat.

🔁 Intro & Outro Skills

Intro and Outro skills are what make team composition matter. They're not just damage — they're your primary source of team-wide buffs.

  • Outro Skill: the outgoing character's parting gift. Examples: Shorekeeper's Outro gives +23% Spectro DMG for 14s; Verina's Outro buffs the entire team's ATK; Mortefi's Outro buffs Heavy Attack DMG for Jiyan.
  • Intro Skill: the incoming character's entrance. Often deals damage and applies an element-specific debuff or self-buff. Intro Skill damage is usually modest — the real value is the Outro buff it carries.
  • Swapping without full Concerto still works — it just doesn't trigger Intro/Outro. You can quick-swap to dodge or reposition, but you lose the buff chain.
🔰 Building around Outros: When choosing teammates, look at their Outro descriptions. If your Main DPS deals Spectro DMG, pair them with Shorekeeper (Spectro Outro buff). If they rely on Basic Attacks, Sanhua's Outro buffs Basic ATK DMG by 25%. This synergy is more important than element matching. See our Tier List → for recommended team comps.

💨 Dodge Counter & Parry

Wuthering Waves rewards mechanical skill. Two techniques separate good players from great ones:

  • Dodge Counter: dodge at the exact moment an enemy attacks and you'll trigger a special counter-attack. The timing window is generous (roughly 0.3s), and the counter deals significantly more damage than a normal dodge. Dodge Counters also generate extra Concerto Energy.
  • Parry (Clash): certain enemy attacks glow with a golden circle — these can be parried by attacking into them. A successful parry staggers the enemy briefly, opening a damage window. Bosses often have 2-3 parryable attacks per cycle.
  • Stamina management: dodging costs stamina. If you run out, you can't dodge. Don't spam dodge — time them. Characters with high combat uptime benefit from stamina food or Echo substats that reduce dodge cost.
