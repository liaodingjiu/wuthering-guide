💎 Gacha & Pity System

Wuthering Waves uses a pity-based gacha similar to Genshin Impact. Here's exactly how it works:

  • 80 pulls to hard pity — you are guaranteed a 5-star within 80 pulls on any character banner. Soft pity kicks in around pull 65, where your 5-star rate increases dramatically with each subsequent pull.
  • 50/50 on first pity — when you hit pity, there's a 50% chance of getting the featured limited character, and a 50% chance of getting a standard 5-star. If you lose the 50/50, your next pity is guaranteed to be the featured character.
  • 160 pulls = worst case — in the absolute worst scenario (lose 50/50 at 80, then go to 80 again), you need 160 pulls to guarantee a limited character. Realistically, most players get theirs between 70–150 pulls.
  • Pity carries over — your pull count transfers between limited character banners of the same type. Weapon banner pity is separate and caps at 80 with no 50/50.
💡 Pro tip: As a new player, prioritize characters over weapons. A new character unlocks team variety and playstyles. Signature weapons are 15-25% stronger than free alternatives — meaningful, but not worth skipping a character for. Use the Tier List → to decide which banner to pull on.

🔮 Echo System Explained

Echoes are Wuthering Waves' equivalent of artifacts or relics — equipable monster skills that provide stats and set bonuses. Understanding this system is the single biggest power spike you'll get outside of pulling characters.

  • 5 echo slots per character: one Cost 4 (boss echo), two Cost 3 (elite echoes), and two Cost 1 (common echoes). The Cost 4 echo provides an active skill you can use in combat.
  • Sonata Effects (set bonuses): equip 2 echoes from the same set for a small bonus, or 5 for the full effect. For example, Sun-sinking Eclipse gives +10% Havoc DMG at 2-piece and up to +30% at 5-piece. Getting the full 5-piece bonus is always the goal.
  • Main stat vs. substats: the main stat is fixed by echo slot. Substats are randomly rolled. You reveal substats using Tuners — don't spend Tuners on echoes with bad main stats, and stop investing once you see 2+ dead substats.
  • Experience transfers: when you feed a leveled echo into a new one, 80% of the EXP carries over. This means you can be aggressive about leveling promising echoes to see their early substats — you won't lose much if they're bad.
⚡ Substats matter more than main stats: A Cost 4 Echo with CRIT Rate main stat and CRIT DMG + ATK% substats will outperform one with perfect main stat but flat DEF and HP substats. Farm until you hit at least one CRIT substat on every echo piece before moving on. Use our Echo database → to find the right set for your character.

⚔️ Combat Fundamentals

Wuthering Waves combat is fast and skill-based. Unlike turn-based gachas, your mechanical skill directly impacts your damage output. Here are the mechanics that matter most:

  • Intro & Outro Skills: when you swap characters, the incoming character triggers their Intro Skill and the outgoing character triggers their Outro Skill. Outro Skills often provide team-wide buffs — for example, Verina's Outro buffs the entire team's ATK, while Shorekeeper's Outro buffs Spectro DMG by 23%. Rotate intentionally, not randomly.
  • Concerto Energy: each character generates Concerto by dealing damage and using skills. When Concerto is full, your next swap triggers both Intro and Outro. Building Concerto quickly on your support and swapping to your DPS is the core rotation loop.
  • Dodge counter & parry: a well-timed dodge triggers a dodge counter — a special attack that deals bonus damage. Certain enemy attacks can be parried by attacking into them, stunning the enemy briefly. Mastering these two mechanics will double your effective DPS in boss fights.
  • Forte Circuit: every character has a unique Forte gauge that powers their strongest abilities. Read your main DPS's Forte description carefully — it's their core mechanic. For Jinhsi, it's Incandescence stacks; for Camellya, it's Thorn stacks that fuel a special blooming strike.

👥 Team Building Basics

A strong Wuthering Waves team follows a simple formula: 1 Main DPS + 1 Sub-DPS + 1 Support/Healer. The synergy comes from element matching, Outro buff alignment, and rotation speed.

  • Main DPS: your on-field damage dealer. They spend the most time attacking and should have the highest investment (levels, Forte upgrades, best echoes). Examples: Jinhsi (Spectro), Camellya (Havoc), Jiyan (Aero), Changli (Fusion).
  • Sub-DPS: provides off-field damage and quick-swap bursts. Ideally matches or complements your Main DPS's element. Their Outro Skill should buff something your Main DPS uses. Example: Yinlin buffs Electro and Resonance Skill DMG for Jinhsi; Mortefi buffs Heavy Attack DMG for Jiyan.
  • Support/Healer: keeps the team alive and provides ATK or CRIT buffs. Verina and Shorekeeper are the two universal top-tier supports — they fit into virtually any team. Baizhi is the budget alternative.
  • Element matching bonus: having two characters of the same element provides an elemental resonance effect. For example, double Spectro increases Spectro DMG for the whole team.

📅 Daily Routine & Waveplate Spending

Waveplates (resin/stamina) cap at 240 and regenerate at a rate of 1 per 6 minutes. Never let them cap — log in at least twice a day to spend them. Here's the optimal spending priority:

  • ① Weekly Bosses (3/week): the highest priority. Weekly bosses drop materials needed for Forte skill upgrades beyond level 6. You can claim 3 per week. Don't skip these — Forte materials are time-gated and you'll regret falling behind.
  • ② Ascension & Upgrade Materials: spend Waveplates on world bosses and Forgery Challenges to get the materials your active team needs. Focus on one character at a time — spreading resources across 4+ characters is the most common new player mistake.
  • ③ Tacet Fields (Echo farming): once your team is leveled, Tacet Fields are your primary echo source. They guarantee echoes of a specific Sonata set and have boosted drop rates for Cost 3 and Cost 4 echoes. Run these after you've done weekly bosses and ascension farming.
  • ④ Shell Credits: you will run out of credits at endgame. Leveling a character from 1-90 costs over 1 million credits. Don't farm credits early, but don't ignore them entirely — a couple of credit runs per week add up.
⏰ Time-saving tip: You can condense Waveplates into Crystal Solvents from the inventory menu. This lets you store up to 480 extra Waveplates for days when you have time to play. Always condense before logging off if you're near the cap.

🔧 Weapon Progression

Weapons are straightforward but high-impact. The right weapon for your character can be a 15-25% damage increase over the wrong one.

  • Signature weapons: each limited 5-star character releases with a matching signature weapon on the weapon banner. These are 15-25% stronger than the best free alternative. Pull for them only if you're a spender or really love the character.
  • Standard 5-star weapons: available from the standard banner and as off-banner pulls. Emerald of Genesis (Sword), Lustrous Razor (Broadblade), and Cosmic Ripples (Rectifier) are all solid generalist options that work on multiple characters.
  • Crafted & Battle Pass weapons: good budget alternatives. The BP weapons are roughly on par with standard 5-stars at high refinement. Crafted weapons are weaker but essentially free.
  • Weapon type matters: each character can only equip their specific weapon type. Jinhsi uses Broadblades; Camellya uses Swords; Verina uses Rectifiers. You can filter our weapon database by type → to find all compatible options.

📈 Ascension & Character Leveling

Leveling a character from 1 to 90 takes approximately 2-3 weeks of optimized Waveplate spending. Here's the roadmap:

  • Phase 1 (Lv.1 → 40): cheap and fast. Do this immediately for any character you plan to use. Costs basic overworld materials and a few boss drops.
  • Phase 2 (Lv.40 → 60): requires the first significant boss material investment. Start farming the character's specific world boss. Each character detail page lists their required boss — for example, Jinhsi needs materials from Mourning Aix.
  • Phase 3 (Lv.60 → 80): the grind begins. Boss material requirements double. Pre-farm materials before pulling if you're targeting a specific upcoming character — use our database to check requirements ahead of time.
  • Phase 4 (Lv.80 → 90): the final push. Expensive but worth it for your Main DPS. Support characters can stay at 80 for a while — the stat gain from 80→90 is modest and the resource cost is high.
📋 Pre-farming is the meta: Once a new character is announced, use our character database → to look up their element, weapon type, and recommended echo set. Farm the boss materials, element cores, and echo set before their banner drops. Then you can level them to 90 on day one.

❓ Quick Answers

Should I pull for weapons or characters?
Characters > weapons, always. A new character unlocks team variety, playstyles, and synergy options. Signature weapons are 15-25% DPS gains — meaningful but replaceable. Free and crafted weapons are good enough for all content including Tower of Adversity.

Is the Battle Pass worth buying?
Yes if you're a new player — the raw EXP mats, shell credits, and BP weapon alone justify the $10. For veterans, it's about 9 extra limited pulls for $10, which is still better pull-per-dollar than direct top-ups.

How many free pulls do I get per patch?
Approximately 110 pulls per version as F2P from dailies, events, Tower of Adversity, exploration, and redemption codes. With Lunite Subscription + Battle Pass, roughly 138-152 pulls per patch.

What's the best way to spend real money?
Priority order: Lunite Subscription ($5/mo) > Weekly Subscription ($10, once per patch) > Battle Pass ($10) > Top-up bundles (buy each tier's first-purchase bonus before repeating any tier). Everything else is significantly less efficient.

How long to build a character from scratch?
~2 weeks to Lv.80 with Lv.6+ Forte skills and a passable echo set. Lv.90 + max Forte + optimized echo substats takes 3-5 weeks. Pre-farm ascension materials before pulling to level them day one.
